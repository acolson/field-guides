Part 4 What to do

Day Ideas, Not a Checklist.

Coming soon. The Andy Warhol Museum, the Duquesne Incline, the Phipps Conservatory, the Strip District morning walk, a Pirates game at PNC Park (one of the most beautiful ballparks in America, full stop).

For a three-day couple's trip, the short version: one museum afternoon (Warhol or Carnegie), one incline ride (Duquesne for the view), one Strip District Saturday walk, and a Pirates home game at PNC Park if the schedule cooperates.

A few directions while this section gets researched: Andy Warhol Museum (North Shore — the world's largest single-artist museum), Carnegie Museum of Art (Oakland — dinosaurs at the natural history half are also worth it), Duquesne Incline (the better of the two inclines — Mt Washington station near Altius if you're already going up there for the view), Phipps Conservatory (Oakland, the Victorian glasshouse), PNC Park (River-side ballpark — Pirates are bad but the view is unbeatable, late-August home stand if scheduled), Strip District Saturday morning (the city's signature walk — DeLuca's breakfast, then Penzeys, Stamoolis, the Pittsburgh Public Market).
