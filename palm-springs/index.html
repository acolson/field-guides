Palm Springs, Pride & the High Desert.

A working guide for two travelers over Pride weekend, Thursday through Tuesday: where to eat, where to stay, where to drink, and what to do – across Palm Springs proper and one night up in Joshua Tree & Pioneertown, researched against the publications worth trusting.

Best for4–5 days
Stay nearDowntown or the Uptown Design District
Get aroundRental car (first drive-everything issue)
WhenPalm Springs Pride — Nov 6–8, 2026
The Trip at a Glance

The Shape of It

Palm Springs is small, flat, and built for a car – this is the first Field Guide city where you'll drive between everything. Thursday-to-Tuesday gives you the full Pride weekend (parade Sunday, Nov 8) plus one night up in the high desert. Base downtown or in the Uptown Design District for the festival and the Arenas Road bars, then drive the 50 minutes north for a Pioneertown night before flying home.

Where to Base Yourself

For a couple at Pride, base in the downtown / Uptown Design District – walk to the parade route and the Arenas Road gay-bar strip from a mid-century design hotel (the Kimpton Rowan, Holiday House, and ARRIVE are the picks). Then one night high-desert – the Pioneertown Motel next to Pappy & Harriet's is the move.

Don't Miss

The Pride parade down Palm Canyon (Sunday Nov 8), a poolside afternoon at a mid-century resort, dinner at Workshop Kitchen + Bar or a martini at Bar Cecil, an Arenas Road bar crawl, and the drive up to Pappy & Harriet's in Pioneertown – a lunch at La Copine on the way.

About This Guide

Palm Springs rewards travelers who organize around the pool, the parade, and the drive rather than a checklist. Mornings are for the pool and brunch; afternoons for the parade, the architecture, or the high-desert drive; nights for the cocktail bars and the Arenas strip. This is a gay couple's Pride trip, and the guide treats the gay resorts and the Arenas bars as central, not peripheral.

This guide is structured to match how the trip actually moves. The restaurants section is tiered by critical consensus, with the Joshua Tree & Pioneertown picks grouped for the one-night-out. The hotels are organized by area and vibe. Use the section you need; the sections are independent.

Researched against Palm Springs Life, the MICHELIN Guide California, The Infatuation, Travel + Leisure, Condé Nast Traveler, Time Out, Hotels Above Par, Tales of the Cocktail, and the gay-travel press (Visit Gay Palm Springs, The Palm Springs Guys, Nomadic Boys).
