Part 4 What to do

Day Ideas, Not a Checklist.

Coming soon. The Pride parade, the Palm Springs Aerial Tramway, the mid-century architecture circuit, and a day in Joshua Tree National Park.

For a Pride-weekend couple's trip, the short version: the parade down Palm Canyon Sunday morning, one pool afternoon, one architecture fix (the Kaufmann House drive-by, the Sunnylands tour, or a Modernism Week-style self-tour), the Aerial Tramway up Mount San Jacinto for the view, and a full day in Joshua Tree National Park on the high-desert leg.

A few directions while this section gets researched: Palm Springs Pride (Nov 6–8, 2026 — parade Sunday down Palm Canyon, festival between Amado and Baristo), Palm Springs Aerial Tramway (the rotating car up to 8,500 ft and a 30-degree temperature drop), the architecture (the Kaufmann House, Frey House II, the Frank Sinatra Twin Palms estate, the Visitor Center in Albert Frey's old gas station), Sunnylands (the Annenberg estate and gardens in Rancho Mirage), the Palm Springs Art Museum, and on the high-desert leg, Joshua Tree National Park (the west entrance from the village, Hidden Valley, Keys View, Cholla Cactus Garden). The Eat, Stay, and Drink sections are fully built; start there.
