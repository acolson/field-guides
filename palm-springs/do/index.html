Part 4 What to do

What to Do in Palm Springs, by Area and the Calendar.

A mapped, area-organized guide to twenty-two things to do — Pride first, then mid-century architecture, museums, the tramway, and one day up in the high desert. Framed as scenic drives, historic sites, and quirky desert oddities, not a hiking roundup.

Sources: Palm Springs Pride · the sites themselves · Sunnylands · NPS Joshua Tree · Visit Palm Springs — hours, ticketing, and 2026 Pride logistics verified where posted; confirm the rest.

How to Use This

Pride First, Then Pick an Area.

Pride eats the front half of the trip, so the architecture, the valley, and the high-desert overnight land Monday-Tuesday, with Thursday's arrival good for VillageFest. This section is organized by area, and frames the desert as scenic-and-historic rather than athletic.

22

2
reservation-critical: Sunnylands & Keys Ranch
Free
the architecture drive-by + Noah Purifoy + Pride parade
1
night up in the high desert

The two booking traps to honor: the Sunnylands house tour releases tickets at 9am PT on the 15th of the prior month (so October 15 for November), and the Keys Ranch tour is Recreation.gov-only and posts ~60 days out. Both sell out. Set the reminders when you lock the trip.

Everything else is flexible. The mid-century icons are private homes, so the architecture is a drive-by (or a small-group Mod Squad tour); the Art Museum and the A+D Center are the indoor anchors (closed Mon-Wed). Frame Joshua Tree as a scenic drive with the historic Keys Ranch tour, not a hike — Keys View and the Cholla garden are reachable by car.

The shape of it: Pride Friday-Sunday, an architecture morning, a Sunnylands morning, and one high-desert day-into-night — Joshua Tree's scenic drive, Pioneertown, and Pappy & Harriet's. Set an October 15 alarm for the Sunnylands tour.
1

Pride Sunday: parade in the morning, pool in the afternoon.

The parade steps off at 10am down Palm Canyon (curb spot by 9:30, find shade), runs about two hours, then a pool afternoon to recover, then the Arenas block party after dark. Thursday's arrival folds neatly into VillageFest.

2

The architecture morning is mostly free — and mostly from the curb.

The self-guided Las Palmas drive-by circuit (or a Mod Squad small-group tour for context), the A+D Center / Art Museum, and the Elvis Honeymoon Hideaway for the one interior you can reliably get into. Cap with Moorten if it's not Wednesday.

3

Sunnylands is the on-brand valley morning — book it October 15.

The Annenberg estate's historic house tour is the most on-brand stop of the trip, but tickets release at 9am PT on the 15th of the prior month and sell out. The gardens are free and walk-up; pair with Cabot's Pueblo.

4

The Joshua Tree day is a drive, not a hike.

Up ~50 minutes: the scenic drive (Keys View + Cholla + Skull Rock), the Keys Ranch tour if you booked it, then Noah Purifoy and the Crochet Museum, then Pioneertown and dinner at Pappy & Harriet's. Sleep up north, detour to the Integratron or Cabot's on the way back.

Pride Weekend

Pride Weekend · 3 stops.

The reason you're here, and the center of gravity for the trip. The festival runs Friday-Sunday on Palm Canyon and the Arenas District; the parade is Sunday morning; the Arenas block party is the free nightly heart of it. Realistically this fills the front half of the trip.

Pride Nov 6-8

Greater Palm Springs Pride Festival

Three days of stages, marketplace, and nightly block parties down the spine of downtown.

Pride Weekend Pride The 40th Annual

The festival footprint covers Palm Canyon Drive (Amado to Baristo), Museum Way, and the historic Arenas District. Multiple stages, a big exhibitor marketplace, and a different block party each night. The 40th edition in 2026, themed Be Included — the center of gravity the whole weekend orbits.

Palm Springs Pride Parade

Pride Weekend Pride 225+ Entries

Palm Springs

Palm Springs · 10 stops.

The architecture, the tramway, the museums, and the village — most of it walkable or a short drive downtown. The mid-century houses are private (drive-by or a small-group tour); the Art Museum and A+D Center are the indoor anchors. Note: the Art Museum is closed Mon-Wed, Moorten closed Wed.

Attraction $$

Palm Springs Aerial Tramway

A slowly spinning glass car climbs 6,000 feet to pine forest and a 30-degree temperature drop.

Palm Springs Attraction World's Largest Rotating Car

The world's largest rotating tram car turns through a full rotation on the ~10-minute ride up Chino Canyon, so both sides get the view. The Mountain Station sits at 8,516 feet with observation decks, restaurants, and a natural-history museum. No hiking required; the view is the thing.

Tramway Gas Station (Visitor Center)

Frey's swooping wedge canopy is the city's most photographed front door.

Palm Springs Architecture Free Albert Frey, 1965

Albert Frey and Robson Chambers designed this former Enco station in 1965 — that dramatic cantilevered, hyperbolic-paraboloid canopy is pure desert modernism. Now the official visitor center, on the National Register since 2015. Free, quick, the obvious first stop if you're driving up the mountain.

Palm Springs Art Museum + A+D Center

A real art museum downtown, plus a design annex inside a 1961 modernist landmark.

The Mid-Century Drive-By Circuit

The icons are private homes — so you see them the way locals do, from the street.

Palm Springs Architecture Free The Famous Houses, from the Curb

A drive-by loop through Las Palmas and Southridge: the Kaufmann Desert House (Neutra, 1946 — heavily screened), Frank Sinatra's Twin Palms, the Elrod House (Lautner, the Bond house, gated), and the Bob Hope House (Lautner's volcano dome). Manage expectations — several are fully screened. For interiors you'd need Modernism Week or a tour.

Moorten Botanical Garden & Cactarium

A one-acre, gloriously eccentric desert garden the same family has run since 1938.

Palm Springs Garden $5 The 1938 Cactus Garden

Chester and Patricia Moorten opened this arboretum in 1938; it's still family-run. The Cactarium — a word they coined — is a greenhouse of rare specimens. Small, idiosyncratic, deeply Palm Springs: paths wind past thousands of cacti in under an hour. The most charming low-effort stop in town.

The Wider Valley

The Wider Valley · 2 stops.

Two on-brand half-day stops a short drive out: the Annenberg estate at Sunnylands (book the house tour the moment November releases, on October 15) and Cabot's hand-built pueblo in Desert Hot Springs.

Historic $$

Sunnylands Center & Gardens

The Annenbergs' pink mid-century retreat, where presidents summited — the most on-brand stop of the trip.

The Valley Historic The Annenberg Estate

The 25,000-sq-ft Annenberg winter home is a mid-century landmark that hosted presidents and royalty; the 90-minute Historic House Tour walks the art and the rooms. The free Center & Gardens — nine acres of desert plantings — needs no ticket. The house tour sells out: set a reminder for October 15.

Cabot's Pueblo Museum

One man's four-story, hand-built pueblo of salvaged materials — gloriously strange.

The Valley Historic $16 A Hand-Built Hopi-Style Pueblo

Cabot Yerxa spent decades hand-building this Hopi-inspired pueblo from reclaimed materials: four stories, 35 rooms, 150 windows, almost nothing square. Guided and audio tours walk the warren of rooms and tell his adventurer-artist story. A genuine desert oddity with real history, ~20 minutes north.

The High Desert

The High Desert · 7 stops.

The one night up north, ~50 minutes away. Frame Joshua Tree as a scenic drive (Keys View, Cholla, Skull Rock) plus the historic Keys Ranch tour, then Pioneertown's movie-set Mane Street and the desert oddities — Noah Purifoy, the Integratron, the Crochet Museum. Pappy & Harriet's is dinner.

Scenic Drive $30/car

Joshua Tree National Park — The Scenic Drive

A drive-through greatest-hits, no hiking required.

High Desert Scenic Drive $30/car The Iconic Stops, No Hiking

Frame it as a scenic drive with photo stops. Enter at the West Entrance and string together Hidden Valley (a flat 1-mile loop, optional), Keys View (the highest overlook, reachable entirely by car — Coachella Valley and the San Andreas Fault below), the Cholla Cactus Garden boardwalk, and Skull Rock, visible from the road.

Keys Ranch Guided Tour

The park's hidden homestead — a preserved ranch you can only see with a ranger.

High Desert Historic $20 Ranger-Led, Reservation-Only

The Keys family homesteaded here for 60 years; the ranch survives with its house, schoolhouse, store, and rusting equipment frozen in place. The only way in is a 90-120 min ranger-led walk (about a half-mile, easy). The historic heart of the park and the most on-brand JT experience — but it sells out.

Pioneertown

A 1946 live-in Western movie set that's still a town, with Pappy's at the end of the street.

High Desert Historic Free A Working 1946 Western Set

Actor Dick Curtis built Pioneertown in 1946 as a living movie set — Roy Rogers and Gene Autry were among the investors. Mane Street (on the National Register) is still pedestrian-only and unpaved by charter; wander the false-front facades and shops. At the end sits Pappy & Harriet's, the obvious dinner.

Noah Purifoy Outdoor Desert Art Museum

A 10-acre field of junk-art monuments out in the open desert.

High Desert Attraction Free 10 Acres of Assemblage

Noah Purifoy spent his last 15 years building large-scale assemblage sculptures from salvaged material across 10 acres of Mojave — over 100 works, weathering in the open. Free, self-guided, unlike anything else: part outdoor museum, part haunted junkyard, wholly serious art. ~10 minutes from Joshua Tree town.

The Integratron

A 1950s all-wood dome an engineer built for rejuvenation, on instructions he said came from Venus.

High Desert Quirky $63 A 1950s Sound-Bath Dome

George Van Tassel built this acoustically perfect all-wood dome in the 1950s, claiming it drew on Tesla and telepathic instructions from extraterrestrials. Today it hosts 60-minute sound baths — crystal bowls played live in the resonant chamber. Pure high-desert woo and a genuine 20th-century oddity, in Landers.

Day Ideas & Skips

Day Ideas, and What to Skip.

A few ready-made one-area outings, and the things you can skip.

Thursday (arrival). VillageFest on Palm Canyon for dinner-by-stall, a quick Forever Marilyn photo, and the Arenas opening night if you have energy.

Pride Sunday. The parade at 10am, a long pool afternoon, then back into the Arenas District after dark.

The architecture morning. The Las Palmas drive-by (or a Mod Squad tour) + the A+D Center / Art Museum + the Elvis Honeymoon Hideaway, capped with Moorten (not Wednesday).

The Joshua Tree overnight. The scenic drive + Keys Ranch + Noah Purifoy + the Crochet Museum + Pioneertown + dinner at Pappy & Harriet's. The Integratron sound bath (Thu-Sun) or Cabot's Pueblo on the way back.

Skip: the paid Cabazon Dinosaurs museum (the giants are free from the lot), chasing wristband circuit parties (the free Arenas block party is the move), expecting to get inside the icon houses (private/screened outside Modernism Week), and the $1,300 private Integratron sessions (the ~$63 shared sound bath is the same dome).
