Toronto, A Long Weekend by Train.

A working guide for two travelers, Friday through Monday: where to eat, where to stay, where to drink, and what to do – organized by neighborhood and time of day, researched against the publications worth trusting.

The Trip at a Glance

The Shape of It

Toronto is bigger than it walks, but smaller than it looks on a map. Three nights gets you four dinners, a long Saturday walking afternoon, and a Sunday brunch before the train back. Stay near Union Station, King West, or the Financial District and you can hit most of downtown on foot, plus a 10-minute Uber to Little Italy, Yorkville, or Ossington.

Where to Base Yourself

For two travelers arriving at Union Station, the right hotel neighborhood is downtown south of Bloor – the Financial District for the train-easy walk, King West for the dinner-energy walk, or Yorkville for the quieter, prettier streets. From any of those, Chinatown, Kensington, Little Italy, and the Distillery District are all 15-minute walks or sub-$10 Ubers.

Don't Miss

A peameal bacon sandwich at St. Lawrence Market, dinner at one of the Tier 1 restaurants (Alo or Edulis for the splurge, Quetzal or Bar Isabel for the room, Sunny's for the discovery), a late-night Cantonese stop at Hong Shing after the train, and one slow walk through Kensington Market on a Saturday afternoon.

About This Guide

Toronto rewards travelers who organize around neighborhood and time of day rather than ticking off a checklist. The Financial District at lunch is a different city from Little Italy at dinner; Kensington Market on a Saturday afternoon is not King West on a Friday night.

This guide is structured to match how a three-night trip actually moves. The restaurants section is tiered by critical consensus – Tier 1 is what every publication agrees on, Tier 5 is breakfast and lunch organized by where you'll already be. Use the section you need; the sections are independent.

Researched against Toronto Life's Best New and 100 Best, the New York Times' 36 Hours, Canada's 100 Best, the Michelin Guide Toronto, Air Canada enRoute's Best New Restaurants, The Infatuation, Condé Nast Traveler, blogTO, the Toronto Star, and Foodism CA.
