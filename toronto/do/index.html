Day ideas, not a checklist.

Coming soon. The museums worth the line, the neighborhoods worth walking, the ferry to the Toronto Islands when the weather agrees.

For a three-night couple's trip, the short version: one museum afternoon (AGO or ROM), one long walk (Kensington into Chinatown, or the Distillery loop), one Toronto Islands ferry day when the weather agrees, and one show night at Massey Hall or Roy Thomson. Everything else is flex.

A few directions while this section gets researched: Art Gallery of Ontario (the Frank Gehry building, the Canadian collection, the Thomson masterpieces), Royal Ontario Museum (the Daniel Libeskind Crystal entrance, the dinosaurs, the bat cave), Distillery District (cobbled Victorian industrial blocks, galleries, Cluny Bistro), Toronto Islands (15-minute ferry from Jack Layton Terminal – bike rentals at Centre Island, beach at Hanlan's Point), Kensington Market (the Saturday walk that connects most of this guide), CN Tower (yes really, but only for the EdgeWalk if you're into that).
