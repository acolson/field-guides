Part 04 What to do

Days, not checklists.

Coming soon. The Freedom Trail without the kitsch, the museums worth the line, neighborhoods worth walking. Organized as day ideas — a morning, an afternoon, a fallback — rather than a ranked list of attractions.

The non-negotiables, until this is built: walk the North End, see one museum (Gardner if you have to pick one), do the Freedom Trail from the Common as far as Paul Revere's house, take an afternoon for the Harborwalk from the ICA. Everything else is bonus.

A few specifics worth searching: the Isabella Stewart Gardner on a Thursday evening for the music, Mapparium in the Christian Science Center (a 30-foot stained glass globe you walk inside), the Black Heritage Trail for the version of Boston history the Freedom Trail skips, and the Charles River Esplanade for a walk that doesn't feel like sightseeing.
