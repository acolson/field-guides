Part 02 Where to stay

Hotels, by neighborhood and vibe.

In progress. Hotels don't lend themselves to the same cross-publication consensus methodology that restaurants do — there's no equivalent of Eater's Best Of for hotels. Working on a different framework for this section.

For now, the short version: stay near the South End, Back Bay, or Boston Common. From any of those, you can walk to the North End in 15 minutes, Lyft to the Seaport in 10, and reach Fenway by T in one stop. Anywhere in the financial district is fine; anywhere past the Pike or out by the airport is a mistake.

A few names worth searching while this section gets researched: The Newbury (Back Bay, classic), The Whitney Hotel (Beacon Hill, small), Raffles Boston (Back Bay, new luxury), The Liberty (Beacon Hill, in a former jail — yes really), The Boxer (West End, budget-friendly), The Inn at St. Botolph (South End, residential feel). The Eat section is fully built out; start there.
